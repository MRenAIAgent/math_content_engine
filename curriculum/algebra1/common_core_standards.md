# Common Core State Standards - Algebra 1

## Overview

Algebra 1 covers standards from four conceptual categories:
1. Number & Quantity (N)
2. Algebra (A)
3. Functions (F)
4. Statistics & Probability (S)

---

## 1. Seeing Structure in Expressions (A-SSE)

### Cluster A: Interpret the structure of expressions

**A-SSE.A.1** - Interpret expressions that represent a quantity in terms of its context.
- A.1a: Interpret parts of an expression, such as terms, factors, and coefficients.
- A.1b: Interpret complicated expressions by viewing one or more of their parts as a single entity.

**A-SSE.A.2** - Use the structure of an expression to identify ways to rewrite it.
- Example: See x⁴ – y⁴ as (x²)² – (y²)², recognizing it as a difference of squares.

### Cluster B: Write expressions in equivalent forms to solve problems

**A-SSE.B.3** - Choose and produce an equivalent form of an expression to reveal properties.
- A.3a: Factor a quadratic expression to reveal the zeros of the function.
- A.3b: Complete the square to reveal maximum/minimum value.
- A.3c: Use properties of exponents to transform exponential expressions.

**A-SSE.B.4** - Derive the formula for the sum of a finite geometric series.

---

## 2. Arithmetic with Polynomials & Rational Expressions (A-APR)

### Cluster A: Perform arithmetic operations on polynomials

**A-APR.A.1** - Understand that polynomials form a system analogous to integers.
- Perform addition, subtraction, and multiplication of polynomials.

### Cluster B: Understand the relationship between zeros and factors

**A-APR.B.2** - Know and apply the Remainder Theorem.
- For polynomial p(x), p(a) is the remainder when dividing by (x - a).

**A-APR.B.3** - Identify zeros of polynomials when suitable factorizations are available.
- Use zeros to construct a rough graph of the function.

### Cluster C: Use polynomial identities to solve problems

**A-APR.C.4** - Prove polynomial identities and use them.
- Example: (a² + b²)² = (a² - b²)² + (2ab)² generates Pythagorean triples.

**A-APR.C.5** - Know and apply the Binomial Theorem for (x + y)ⁿ.

### Cluster D: Rewrite rational expressions

**A-APR.D.6** - Rewrite simple rational expressions in different forms.
- Write a(x)/b(x) in the form q(x) + r(x)/b(x).

**A-APR.D.7** - Understand that rational expressions form a system analogous to rational numbers.

---

## 3. Creating Equations (A-CED)

### Cluster A: Create equations that describe numbers or relationships

**A-CED.A.1** - Create equations and inequalities in one variable.
- Include linear, quadratic, simple rational, and exponential equations.

**A-CED.A.2** - Create equations in two or more variables.
- Graph equations on coordinate axes with labels and scales.

**A-CED.A.3** - Represent constraints by equations or inequalities.
- Represent constraints by systems of equations/inequalities.
- Interpret solutions as viable or nonviable in modeling context.

**A-CED.A.4** - Rearrange formulas to highlight a quantity of interest.
- Use the same reasoning as in solving equations.

---

## 4. Reasoning with Equations & Inequalities (A-REI)

### Cluster A: Understand solving equations as a process of reasoning

**A-REI.A.1** - Explain each step in solving a simple equation.
- Construct a viable argument to justify a solution method.

**A-REI.A.2** - Solve simple rational and radical equations.
- Give examples showing how extraneous solutions may arise.

### Cluster B: Solve equations and inequalities in one variable

**A-REI.B.3** - Solve linear equations and inequalities in one variable.
- Include equations with coefficients represented by letters.

**A-REI.B.4** - Solve quadratic equations in one variable.
- A.4a: Use completing the square to derive the quadratic formula.
- A.4b: Solve by inspection, square roots, completing the square, quadratic formula, and factoring.
- Recognize when the quadratic formula gives complex solutions.

### Cluster C: Solve systems of equations

**A-REI.C.5** - Prove that replacing one equation by a sum preserves solutions.

**A-REI.C.6** - Solve systems of linear equations exactly and approximately.
- Focus on pairs of linear equations in two variables.

**A-REI.C.7** - Solve a simple system consisting of a linear equation and a quadratic equation.

**A-REI.C.8** - Represent a system of linear equations as a single matrix equation.

**A-REI.C.9** - Find the inverse of a matrix if it exists.
- Use the inverse to solve systems of linear equations.

### Cluster D: Represent and solve equations graphically

**A-REI.D.10** - Understand that the graph of an equation is the set of all its solutions.

**A-REI.D.11** - Explain why the x-coordinates of intersection points satisfy f(x) = g(x).
- Find solutions approximately using technology.

**A-REI.D.12** - Graph the solutions to a linear inequality as a half-plane.
- Graph the solution set to a system of linear inequalities.

## 5. Functions - Interpreting Functions (F-IF)

### Cluster A: Understand the concept of a function

**F-IF.A.1** - Understand that a function assigns exactly one output to each input.

**F-IF.A.2** - Use function notation, evaluate functions for inputs in their domains.

**F-IF.A.3** - Recognize that sequences are functions with domain of natural numbers.

### Cluster B: Interpret functions that arise in applications

**F-IF.B.4** - Interpret key features of graphs and tables (intercepts, intervals, max/min, symmetry).

**F-IF.B.5** - Relate the domain of a function to its graph.

**F-IF.B.6** - Calculate and interpret the average rate of change.

### Cluster C: Analyze functions using different representations

**F-IF.C.7** - Graph functions expressed symbolically and show key features.
- C.7a: Graph linear and quadratic functions, show intercepts, maxima, minima.
- C.7b: Graph square root, cube root, and piecewise-defined functions.
- C.7e: Graph exponential and logarithmic functions.

**F-IF.C.8** - Write a function defined by an expression in different equivalent forms.
- C.8a: Factor and complete the square for quadratic functions.
- C.8b: Use properties of exponents to interpret exponential functions.

**F-IF.C.9** - Compare properties of two functions represented in different ways.

## 6. Functions - Building Functions (F-BF)

### Cluster A: Build a function that models a relationship

**F-BF.A.1** - Write a function that describes a relationship.
- A.1a: Determine an explicit expression or recursive process from context.
- A.1b: Combine standard function types using arithmetic operations.

**F-BF.A.2** - Write arithmetic and geometric sequences recursively and explicitly.

### Cluster B: Build new functions from existing functions

**F-BF.B.3** - Identify the effect on the graph of replacing f(x) with f(x) + k, kf(x), f(kx), and f(x + k).

**F-BF.B.4** - Find inverse functions.

---

## 7. Functions - Linear, Quadratic, and Exponential Models (F-LE)

### Cluster A: Construct and compare linear, quadratic, and exponential models

**F-LE.A.1** - Distinguish between situations modeled by linear vs exponential functions.

**F-LE.A.2** - Construct linear and exponential functions from graphs, tables, or descriptions.

**F-LE.A.3** - Observe that exponential functions eventually exceed polynomial functions.

### Cluster B: Interpret expressions for functions

**F-LE.B.5** - Interpret parameters in linear and exponential functions in context.

---

## 8. Statistics - Interpreting Categorical & Quantitative Data (S-ID)

### Cluster A: Summarize, represent, and interpret data

**S-ID.A.1** - Represent data with plots on the real number line (dot plots, histograms, box plots).

**S-ID.A.2** - Use statistics appropriate to the shape of the distribution.

**S-ID.A.3** - Interpret differences in shape, center, and spread; account for outliers.

### Cluster B: Summarize, represent, and interpret two categorical/quantitative variables

**S-ID.B.5** - Summarize categorical data in two-way frequency tables.

**S-ID.B.6** - Represent data on two quantitative variables and describe relationships.
- B.6a: Fit a function to data; use functions fitted to data to solve problems.
- B.6b: Informally assess the fit of a function by plotting residuals.
- B.6c: Fit a linear function for scatter plots that suggest a linear association.

### Cluster C: Interpret linear models

**S-ID.C.7** - Interpret the slope and intercept of a linear model.

**S-ID.C.8** - Compute and interpret the correlation coefficient.

**S-ID.C.9** - Distinguish between correlation and causation.

## Number and Quantity (N-Q)

### Reason quantitatively and use units to solve problems

**N-Q.A.1** - Use units as a way to understand problems and guide the solution.

**N-Q.A.2** - Define appropriate quantities for the purpose of descriptive modeling.

**N-Q.A.3** - Choose a level of accuracy appropriate to limitations on measurement.
